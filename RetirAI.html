About the lump-sum option
Swiss BVG/LPP pension funds allow you to take part or all of your retirement capital as a lump sum instead of a lifelong annuity. The conversion rate is currently ~4.8%, meaning €210,000 in capital = €840/mo annuity. If you take capital, you manage it yourself — a 4% drawdown over 25 years gives ~€700/mo, but runs out. Lump-sum withdrawals are taxed at a reduced rate (~6.5% in most cantons). You must notify your pension fund 3 years before retirement.

Pillar 1 — State
Mandatory public pension. Funded by salary contributions. Every employee has this.
e.g. CNAP (LU) · CNAV (FR) · AVS (CH)
Pillar 2 — Occupational
Employer pension fund. Often automatic, varies by company and country.
e.g. LPP/BVG (CH) · AGIRC-ARRCO (FR) · Pension complémentaire (LU)
Pillar 3 — Private
Voluntary personal savings. Tax-advantaged in most countries. Optional.
e.g. 3a account (CH) · PER (FR) · Assurance-vie (LU)
