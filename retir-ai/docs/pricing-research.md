# Prevista Pricing Research — Competitive Landscape & Positioning

*Research date: April 2026*
*Target market: Luxembourg-based expats with multi-country careers (FR/CH/LU)*

---

## 1. The Competitive Landscape

Prevista occupies a unique niche — there is **no direct competitor** offering a self-service, multi-country pension planning platform for European expats. The competitive landscape is fragmented across four categories:

### Category A: DIY Retirement Planning Software (US-focused)

These are the closest product analogues but serve a fundamentally simpler problem (single-country, single tax system).

#### Boldin (formerly NewRetirement)
- **Price**: $144/yr (~€12/mo billed annually)
- **Free tier**: Basic planner with ~150 inputs, no account linking, no Monte Carlo
- **Paid tier**: ~250 inputs, account linking, Monte Carlo analysis, digital coach, live Q&A
- **Advisor tier**: $2,800 flat fee for CFP access
- **Market**: US only, single country
- **Source**: [Boldin Pricing](https://www.boldin.com/retirement/pricing/)

#### ProjectionLab
- **Price**: $129/yr (~€11/mo) or $12/mo monthly, $1,199 lifetime
- **Free tier**: Ad-hoc projections, Monte Carlo — but no data persistence (lost on logout)
- **Paid tier**: Data persistence, tax estimation, scenario comparisons, Roth conversion modelling
- **Pro tier**: $45/mo for financial advisors managing multiple clients
- **Market**: US only, single country
- **Source**: [ProjectionLab Pricing](https://projectionlab.com/pricing)

#### Empower (formerly Personal Capital)
- **Price**: Free planning tools, but monetises via AUM (0.49%-0.89% of assets)
- **Market**: US only, investment-focused, not pension-specific
- **Source**: [Empower Retirement Planner](https://www.empower.com/tools/retirement-planner)

**Key takeaway**: The US market has established a **$10-15/mo price point** for sophisticated retirement planning tools. However, these solve a single-country problem. Prevista solves a multi-country problem that is orders of magnitude more complex.

---

### Category B: UK Pension Consolidation Platforms

These platforms consolidate scattered UK pensions into one pot. They're relevant because they solve the "fragmented pensions" problem — but only within one country.

#### PensionBee
- **Price**: 0.50% - 0.95% annual management fee on assets (not subscription)
- **Free**: Pension finding/consolidation service is free
- **Revenue model**: AUM-based (takes a cut of your pension pot annually)
- **Example**: On a £100K pot at 0.50%, that's £500/yr (~€580/yr)
- **Market**: UK only, pension consolidation + investment management
- **Source**: [PensionBee Fees](https://www.pensionbee.com/uk/fees)

**Key takeaway**: PensionBee demonstrates that pension services can command **€500+/yr** via AUM fees. Their service is simpler (single country, consolidation only) but validates high willingness-to-pay for pension management.

---

### Category C: Multi-Country Pension Discovery Services

These are the closest to Prevista's cross-border positioning but offer different services.

#### PensionHunter (launched April 2026)
- **Price**: $99/search (single country) or $199/search (all 11 countries)
- **Success fee**: 15% of recovered pension value
- **Refund**: Full refund if nothing found
- **Coverage**: UK, Australia, Netherlands, Ireland, Canada, Singapore, UAE, Malaysia, South Africa, New Zealand, Hong Kong
- **Notable**: Does NOT cover France, Switzerland, or Luxembourg
- **Service**: AI-powered pension record discovery and claim letter generation. One-time search, not ongoing monitoring.
- **Source**: [PensionHunter Launch](https://natlawreview.com/press-releases/pensionhunter-launches-global-ai-pension-recovery-service-across-11-countries)

#### European Tracking Service (ETS) — findyourpension.eu
- **Price**: Free (EU-funded public service)
- **Coverage**: Currently only Belgium and France share data; Germany, Netherlands, Sweden are members but not yet connected
- **Notable**: Does NOT include Switzerland (non-EU), Luxembourg not yet connected
- **Limitations**: Read-only tracking — shows where your pensions are, no planning, no tax simulation, no family protection
- **Timeline**: Full rollout planned by 2027, currently very limited
- **Funding**: €4.7M EU grant awarded March 2024
- **Source**: [ETS About](https://www.findyourpension.eu/about-ets)

#### National Pension Dashboards
- **Netherlands (mijnpensioenoverzicht.nl)**: Free, government-run, shows all Dutch pensions in one view. Requires DigiD. Single-country only.
- **UK Pensions Dashboard**: In development, not yet live for consumers.
- **France (info-retraite.fr)**: Free, shows French pension entitlements only.

**Key takeaway**: Government/EU solutions are free but **single-country, read-only, and years from full deployment**. PensionHunter charges $99-199 per search plus 15% success fee for a one-time service. Prevista offers ongoing, multi-country, actionable planning — a fundamentally different value proposition.

### Category D: Expat Financial Advisory Firms

These are the human-service alternatives that Prevista's target users would otherwise use.

#### Blevins Franks
- **Price**: Not published, bespoke per client
- **Service**: Cross-border financial planning for expats in Europe (Spain, France, Portugal, Cyprus, Malta)
- **Model**: Ongoing advisory relationship, likely 1-2% AUM + consultation fees

#### Hoxton Wealth
- **Price**: Not published, bespoke per client
- **Service**: International wealth management app + advisor access, 12,000+ clients
- **App**: Free (account aggregation, retirement tracking), monetises via advisory services
- **Source**: [Hoxton Wealth](https://hoxtonwealth.com/)

#### Blacktower Financial Management
- **Price**: Not published, bespoke per client
- **Service**: 40+ years of expat financial planning across Europe

#### Typical Advisory Costs (industry benchmarks)
- **Consultation**: €200-400/hr
- **AUM fee**: 1-2% annually
- **Pension transfer review**: €2,000-5,000 one-off
- **Ongoing planning**: €3,000-10,000/yr for multi-country situations

**Key takeaway**: Professional cross-border pension advice costs **€3,000-10,000+/yr**. Prevista automates a significant portion of what these advisors do (consolidation, tax simulation, document tracking, gap detection). Even at €240/yr (€19.90/mo), it's **92-97% cheaper** than human advice.

## 3. Pricing Analysis

### Why €9.90/mo is too low

1. **Signals "not serious"** in the Luxembourg market. Average net income is €3,540/mo — €9.90 is what people pay for a streaming app, not a financial planning tool managing €200K+ in pension assets.
2. **Below US single-country tools**. Boldin charges $12/mo for US-only planning. Prevista solves a harder problem across 3+ countries.
3. **Leaves money on the table**. The value delivered (€120/mo gap recovery, €356/mo tax optimisation, family protection across €3.7B in unclaimed pensions) vastly exceeds €9.90.

### Why €19.90/mo is the right entry point

1. **Premium to US tools, but justified by complexity**. Multi-country × multi-language × multi-pillar = genuinely harder to build and more valuable to users.
2. **Still a no-brainer ROI**. If the tax simulation alone identifies a €200/mo saving from residency optimisation, the tool pays for itself in 3 days.
3. **Affordable for the target market**. €19.90 is 0.56% of the median Luxembourg net salary. For the target demographic (€50K-150K+ earners), it's negligible.
4. **Annual discount drives retention**. €199/yr (17% discount) encourages commitment and reduces churn.
5. **Room to grow**. Starting at €19.90 allows future premium tiers (e.g., €39.90 with expert consultations) without cannibalising the base.

### Price sensitivity context

- **Netflix Standard (Luxembourg)**: €13.49/mo — entertainment
- **Spotify Premium**: €11.99/mo — entertainment
- **Boldin PlannerPlus**: ~€12/mo — US retirement planning
- **ProjectionLab Premium**: ~€11/mo — US retirement planning
- **Prevista Pro (proposed)**: €19.90/mo — multi-country pension planning
- **PensionBee (on €100K pot)**: ~€48/mo — UK pension management
- **Financial advisor (annual)**: €250-833/mo — human advice

Prevista at €19.90/mo sits correctly between DIY tools and professional advice — reflecting that it automates what advisors charge thousands for, while being more sophisticated than single-country calculators.
