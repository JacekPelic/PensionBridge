## 1. Go-To-Market Strategy

### The Strategy in One Paragraph

Launch with a free pension calculator that validates the acquisition funnel organically in Luxembourg. Use early metrics (conversion rates, engagement, lead gen clicks) to prove unit economics. Raise a €230K seed round to accelerate via paid acquisition, content, and the BD co-founder's distribution partnerships channel. Expand aggressively across EU migration corridors, adding country engines in greedy TAM-maximizing order until the entire EU + UK + CH (29 countries) is covered by Year 5. Each new country engine unlocks bilateral pension corridors with all already-supported countries, multiplying all three revenue streams. Build a compounding capital-under-referral base that becomes increasingly valuable over time, transforming from a pure SaaS into a financial platform with asset-based recurring revenue.

### Phase 1: Bootstrap — Validate the Funnel (Pre-Investment)

**The standalone calculator is the bootstrap engine.** It's a separate, lightweight tool that proves the acquisition funnel works before investment is needed.

**Why a calculator:**
- Nobody knows Prevista exists, but people ARE searching for answers to specific cross-border pension questions
- Low commitment — no account, no signup, instant value
- Naturally qualifies leads — anyone using a cross-border pension calculator IS the target customer
- The pension calculation engines (FR/CH/LU) are already built and validated

**The funnel:**
1. User finds calculator via SEO or community link
2. Enters basic career details → gets rough pension estimate
3. **CTA 1: "Get detailed report by email"** → email capture = marketing consent → enters drip campaign
4. **CTA 2: "See your complete pension picture"** → directs to main Prevista platform for account creation

**Acquisition in Phase 1 (organic only, no spend):**
- **SEO / Content** — landing pages per country combination and long-tail pension queries (EN first, FR/DE later)
- **Community seeding** — expat forums, Facebook groups, Reddit (r/luxembourg, r/expats), English Forum Switzerland — sharing the calculator as a genuinely useful free tool

**Email funnel design:**
- **Calculator output** shows: estimated monthly pension per country + total
- **Email report** adds new value beyond calculator: breakdown by country, identified risks/gaps for the user's specific profile, and CTA to the platform
- **Drip campaign** (2-3 emails over 2 weeks): educates about risks, gaps, unclaimed pensions → CTA to platform

**What Phase 1 proves:**
- Calculator → email capture conversion rate (target: 25-35%)
- Email → platform signup conversion rate (target: 15-22%)
- Signup → paid conversion rate (target: 8-12%)
- Lead gen click-through rate on product offers (target: 25%+)
- Which country combinations are most searched (informs expansion priority)

These metrics are the evidence for raising investment. Even modest absolute numbers (hundreds of users, dozens of subscribers) are sufficient if the conversion rates hold — because they prove the machine works before you pour money into it.

### Phase 2: Accelerate — Investment Unlocks Growth

The organic baseline proves the funnel. Investment is what turns a working funnel into a growth business. Without investment, Prevista is a lifestyle business doing ~€20-30K ARR by Year 3 on organic alone. With investment, the same funnel scales to €1.70M ARR by Year 3 across the corridor expansion roadmap.

**What changes with investment — three acquisition channels instead of one:**

| Channel | Pre-Investment | Post-Investment | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Organic (SEO + community)** | Only channel. Slow to start. | Accelerated by €50K content investment. More content = more pages ranking = more traffic. | Foundation. Compounds over time. Near-zero marginal cost. Content written in Month 1 still drives traffic in Month 36. |
| **Paid acquisition** | Doesn't exist. | €60-300K/yr in targeted ads. Google Ads on high-intent pension queries, Facebook/LinkedIn targeting expat demographics. | Controllable, predictable. The lever that turns proven unit economics into growth. |
| **Distribution partnerships** | Doesn't exist. | BD co-founder builds relationships with corporate HR departments, expat associations, content partners, mobility consultancies. | High-LTV, leveraged. Each partnership leverages an existing audience — one deal = many signups. |

**Why the model only works with three channels:**

Organic alone caps reach at ~6% of the addressable market over a year. With LU as the only beachhead and ~140K addressable, that's a ceiling worth a few hundred subscribers — viable as a proof of concept, not as a business.

Paid acquisition breaks the reach ceiling. €100 in ads → 1 signup with €55-80 blended expected value (factoring in both subscription probability and lead gen probability). The funnel is already proven to work — ads just pour more people into it.

Distribution partnerships break the trust barrier. Financial tools need credibility. An HR department recommending Prevista to relocating employees, or an expat association featuring it in their newsletter, is worth more than any ad. The BD co-founder owns this channel from day 1; first deals close post-seed.

**What the investment buys (€230K seed):**

**What is *not* in the seed:**

- **Co-founder salaries** — both technical and BD co-founders work on equity until Series A.
- **Country pension engine development** — the technical co-founder builds all 29 country engines (EU27 + UK + CH) over 5 years as part of normal product work, no cash cost. LU/FR/CH are already prototyped.
- **BD hire** — replaced by a BD co-founder who owns distribution partnerships full-time.

**The investor ROI thesis:**

Without investment, Year 3 ARR is small — limited by organic reach in a single Luxembourg beachhead. With €230K invested, Year 3 ARR is €1.70M (base) and Year 5 ARR is €3.67M (base) / €7.22M (upside). By Year 5, Prevista covers the entire EU + UK + CH (29 country engines), serving ~21,200 paying subscribers across all major European migration corridors. The model breaks even monthly at Month 16 and cumulatively at Month 20 — meaning the seed capital bridges the business to self-sustaining profitability with months of runway still untouched. The investment isn't funding losses — it's **buying speed** into a market with proven unit economics, zero direct competitors, and a 17M-person EU addressable ceiling.

### Phase 3: Compound — Three Revenue Streams Grow Together

As the business matures, three compounding effects drive growth beyond what any single channel could achieve:

1. **SEO compounds.** Content published in Year 1 keeps ranking and driving traffic in Year 3+. The library of pension-related content grows with each country added, each corridor opened, each language launched. Organic traffic in Year 3 is typically 3-5x Year 1 for consistent content producers.

2. **Capital under referral compounds.** Users who invest through product referrals in Year 1 are still generating 0.03% commission in Year 5. Their capital has grown through additional contributions and market returns. Each new cohort of investors adds to the base while existing capital compounds. By Year 5, €425M in capital under referral generates ~€127K/yr in pure-margin revenue that requires zero ongoing acquisition spend.

3. **Addressable market expands.** Each new country engine opens new bilateral pension corridors with all already-supported countries, multiplying all three revenue streams simultaneously. The greedy TAM-maximizing rollout (see Section 5) means each engine added unlocks the largest possible marginal market — for example, adding Germany after Spain unlocks ~432K corridor TAM in a single engine. By Year 5, all 29 engines (EU27 + UK + CH) are live and the addressable market is ~12M people.

These three effects mean the business becomes more valuable over time even with flat user acquisition rates. A 5-year-old Prevista with €425M+ in referred capital, 29 country engines, and hundreds of ranking SEO pages has a fundamentally different value profile than a Year 1 startup — and much of that value was created by compounding, not by additional spend.

## 2. Target Market Definition

### Who Is the Target User?

People who have **actually changed where they work** over their career and now have pension entitlements sitting in multiple countries. Specifically:

- **Expats/immigrants** who worked in their home country before relocating to Luxembourg (e.g., Portuguese who worked in Portugal, French who worked in France)
- **Mobile professionals** who moved between countries for career reasons
- **EU institution workers** who had private sector careers before joining EU bodies

**Important distinction:** Cross-border commuters (frontaliers) are NOT the primary market. A German living in Germany and commuting to Luxembourg has their pension contributions in Luxembourg only — they don't have the multi-country problem. The target is people who have **relocated** and accumulated entitlements in 2+ countries.

### Why Luxembourg as the Beachhead

Luxembourg has the highest concentration of multi-country career holders in Europe:
- ~48% of the population is foreign-born
- Large, established immigrant communities from Portugal, France, Belgium, Germany, Italy
- Most first-generation immigrants worked in their home country before relocating
- High income levels (median net salary ~€3,540/mo) support willingness to pay for financial tools
- Small, concentrated market — easier to reach via community channels

## 5. Country Expansion Strategy

### Core Principle: Greedy TAM-Maximizing Sequencing

Expansion is not about "launching in Germany" — it's about **adding the German pension engine**, which makes the calculator useful for anyone who has worked in Germany at any point in their career, regardless of where they live now.

The cross-border pension problem exists along **migration corridors**. Each new country engine unlocks all bilateral corridors with already-supported countries. The same engine added in a different order produces wildly different marginal TAM:

- **Adding Portugal when only LU is supported:** unlocks the PT-LU corridor → ~56K addressable
- **Adding Portugal when LU + FR are supported:** unlocks PT-LU + the massive PT-FR corridor (~600K Portuguese in France) → ~414K addressable

Same engine, **7x the TAM** depending on order. The expansion sequence is therefore optimised greedily — at each step, ship the engine that maximises the sum of newly-unlocked corridor populations across all already-supported countries.

### End State: 29 Countries Live by Year 5

**Prevista's geographic goal is the entire EU + UK + Switzerland — 29 country pension engines** covering the full European multi-country pension landscape. This is achieved over 5 years, with the technical co-founder building engines as part of ongoing product work (no incremental cash cost).

### Engine Build Pace

The schedule is **front-loaded at launch** (three engines live from day one), then proceeds on a quarterly cadence through Year 1, then a steady ~2-month cadence through Year 5. Same 29-country endpoint as earlier plans, hit at Month 58.

Prevista monetises through two complementary streams:

### 6.2 Acquisition Channels

The revenue model depends on three acquisition channels with different economics and scaling characteristics. The organic channel is the bootstrap engine; paid and partnerships are where investment capital creates acceleration.

**Channel 1: Organic (SEO + Community)**

The standalone pension calculator drives organic traffic via search and community sharing.

- **How it works:** Long-tail SEO content (e.g., "French pension calculator for Luxembourg residents") + calculator tool + community seeding in expat forums/groups
- **Characteristics:** Slow start (3-6 months to rank), but compounds — content written in Month 1 keeps driving traffic in Month 24. Near-zero marginal cost.
- **Benchmark:** Niche financial SaaS tools typically reach 3-5% of their addressable market organically within 2 years (source: OpenView SaaS benchmarks, Boldin estimated trajectory)
- **Year 1 vs Year 3:** SEO traffic typically 3-5x higher in Year 3 than Year 1 for consistent content producers

**Channel 2: Paid Acquisition**

Targeted ads on high-intent search queries and social platforms where expats congregate.

- **How it works:** Google Ads on cross-border pension queries (low volume but high intent), Facebook/LinkedIn ads targeting expat demographics in key countries, retargeting calculator visitors who didn't sign up
- **Key economics:**
  - CPC for financial planning keywords in EU: €5-15 (Google Ads Keyword Planner, EU financial services vertical)
  - Expected landing page → calculator conversion: 30-50% (high-intent financial tool benchmark — Unbounce 2025 conversion report)
  - Calculator → email/signup: same funnel rates as organic
  - **Modeled blended CAC: €40/signup (base) / €30 (upside)** — defensible for niche cross-border-pension keywords with effectively zero competitive bidding pressure
- **Why this needs investment:** Organic alone caps reach at ~5% over 2 years. Paid acquisition is the lever that turns a validated funnel into scalable growth. €50K in ad spend at €40 blended CAC = 1,250 new platform signups, of which ~150 convert to paid (12% paid conversion) plus ~38 generate lead gen revenue.
- **Important context:** In the model's 5-year run, paid acquisition contributes only ~10% of total signups — organic and partnerships dominate. CAC optimization matters for unit economics narrative but does not dominate headline ARR.

**Channel 3: Partnerships**

B2B relationships that create bulk user acquisition.

- **How it works:**
  - HR departments of large Luxembourg/EU employers recommend Prevista to relocating employees
  - Relocation agencies include Prevista in their service packages
  - EU institutions (Commission, EIB, ECB, EIF) — high concentration of multi-country career holders
- **Characteristics:** Long sales cycle (3-6 months), but high LTV and low churn once established. Each corporate partnership can bring hundreds of users at once.
- **Benchmark:** B2B financial SaaS partnerships typically take 6-12 months to establish but deliver 30-50% of revenue by Year 3 (Stripe Atlas, Gusto partner programme trajectories)

**Per-signup CAC (paid channel):** **€40 (base) / €30 (upside)**.

This translates to a per-signup LTV:CAC ratio of **1.6× at base parameters** — approaching but not yet at the conventional SaaS 3× target. The honest framing for investors:

> "Paid acquisition is approaching healthy unit economics on a per-signup basis. Critically, paid is only ~10% of total acquisition in the model — organic SEO + distribution partnerships drive the majority. The blended channel mix has healthier economics than this paid-only ratio implies, and CPC optimization post-launch is expected to push the paid ratio toward 2.5-3×."

**Per-paying-subscriber math** (the more conservative cut):
- Effective per-sub CAC = €40 / 12% = **€333**
- Sub LTV = **€492**
- Per-sub ratio = **1.48×** (subscription revenue alone covers acquisition with margin to spare)
- Add lead-gen contribution and the picture improves further

**Key insight for investors:** Even free users have revenue potential through lead gen. Every user acquired, whether they pay for Pro or not, has a positive expected value (€4.50). This subsidises the effective CAC for the paying-subscriber cohort.

### 6.4 Lead Generation — Detailed Model

**How it works:** Users who discover they have a retirement gap are shown financial products (voluntary pension plans, investment funds, life insurance) from partner institutions. If a user invests through a Prevista referral, Prevista earns **0.03% annually on the invested capital** as a trailing commission.

This is a standard financial services affiliate model (comparable to Hargreaves Lansdown platform fees, Nutmeg referral commissions, and insurance comparison site models). The commission is recurring — as long as the capital remains invested, the revenue continues.

**Why this is strategically important:**
- Works on ALL platform users, not just paid subscribers — the free tier is a lead gen machine
- Revenue compounds: each cohort of investors generates ongoing revenue while new cohorts are added
- Near-zero churn: people rarely move retirement investments once placed
- Pure margin: no delivery cost after referral
- Capital grows: additional contributions + market returns increase the commission base over time

**Lead gen funnel assumptions:**

### 6.7 Lead Gen Compounding — The Long-Term Play

At 0.03%, lead gen starts as a small share of revenue. But unlike subscriptions (which churn monthly), invested capital tends to stay and grow through additional contributions and market returns. This creates a compounding revenue base that becomes increasingly valuable over time — and unlike subscription growth, it accumulates **even when no new users are acquired**.

Lead gen is **not the headline revenue stream** — subscriptions dominate at every year of the projection. But it's a structurally durable, asset-based revenue base that grows by compounding rather than by acquisition spend, creating long-term enterprise value that pure subscription SaaS doesn't.

### 6.8 Strategic Implications

1. **Two revenue streams derisk the business.** Subscriptions provide predictable recurring revenue. Lead gen provides compounding long-term value. Neither stream needs to succeed alone.

2. **The free tier generates revenue, not just leads.** Every free user who sees their retirement gap and clicks a product offer has lead gen value (~€4.50 LTV). This means the effective CAC for the platform is lower than it appears — the free tier subsidises its own acquisition cost.

3. **Investment accelerates an already-positive engine.** The organic baseline is modest but validates unit economics. Paid acquisition, content acceleration, and partnership development are how a seed round turns a working funnel into a growth business.

4. **Country expansion is a revenue multiplier on both streams.** Each new country engine adds subscription revenue (larger addressable market) and lead gen revenue (more users seeing gaps + access to that country's financial product partners).

5. **The business becomes more valuable over time.** Unlike pure SaaS where revenue = subscriptions, Prevista's capital under referral grows cumulatively. A 5-year-old Prevista with €425M in referred capital generates ~€127K/yr in pure-margin lead gen revenue that doesn't require any new user acquisition. This is an asset that compounds.

## 7. Competitive Moat

Why this market is defensible once established:

1. **Data lock-in** — every document uploaded increases switching cost (free tier strategy)
2. **Engine complexity** — country-specific pension formulas with EU coordination rules (Regulation 883/2004) are genuinely hard to build and maintain
3. **Content authority** — first mover on cross-border pension SEO in English creates a durable organic acquisition channel
4. **Network effects (weak)** — family sharing feature means one paying user can bring in family members
5. **No direct competitor exists** — government tools are single-country and read-only; US SaaS tools don't cover Europe; advisors cost 92-97% more
