## 1. Context & goal

Prevista's SEO roadmap addresses Trigger 1 with ~24 corridor pages. That channel compounds slowly — organic only reaches meaningful volume in month 9+. The relocation-agency channel is the **offline complement** that closes the zero-to-six-month gap by reaching Profile-A users at the exact moment of highest Trigger-1 intent: *the week they are moving countries*.

**Goal:** 5 active agency partnerships by month 6, producing **~225 trial signups and 50-60 paying subscribers per year** once steady-state under the two-tier partnership model (see Section 4). Contributes ~25-30% of the first-1000-customer target.

**Why this matters now:** relocation agencies are the only commercial party who sees the user *before* pension questions become urgent. Once the move is done, users forget the old employer's name, misfile the last payslip, and the French CARSAT / Agirc-Arrco / Swiss Freizügigkeit trail goes cold. Every month of delay compounds the problem Prevista is designed to solve — and the acquisition cost rises in parallel.

## 2. Why relocation agencies (and not, e.g., corporate HR directly)

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relocation agencies (this plan) | Single partner unlocks 100-500 users/year; already touches users at move moment; under margin pressure and hungry for differentiation | Requires in-person selling; revenue-share dilutes margin | **Primary channel** |
| Corporate HR direct (Amazon LU, Big 4, etc.) | Highest volume if landed | 9-18 month procurement cycle; GDPR/legal hurdles; not reachable without case studies first | Defer to month 12+ |
| Expat associations / employer groups | Warm audiences, low cost | Slow to convert; no in-flight trigger | Secondary (see workshops plan) |
| Paid ads at move-moment (Google "moving to Luxembourg pension") | Fast to launch | Head-term CPCs €5-15; bootstrap budget doesn't support | Defer to post-seed |

Relocation agencies are the best **ratio of reachable-in-bootstrap to qualified-volume-delivered**.

## 4. The value exchange

Prevista operates a **two-tier partnership model**: Founding Partners (first 3) take pilot risk and get commercial upside; Standard Partners (agency #4 onwards) join on simpler, scale-ready terms. The core client benefit — 3 months of free Pro — is identical across both tiers.

### Tier A — Founding Partners (first 3 Tier-2 agencies)

These partners integrate before Prevista has published a case study. They absorb the pilot risk and help validate the channel. In return:

1. **Revenue share on paid conversions.** 30% of first-year subscription + 10% trailing in year 2. Payable for the life of contract.
2. **3 months free Pro for every referred user** (see client benefit below).
3. **Segment exclusivity for 18 months.** No competing Tier-2 agency in the same segment (e.g., LU corporate relocation) gets onboarded until month 19.
4. **Quarterly co-branded founder workshop.** 60-min session delivered to their assignees, which the agency markets as their own client event.
5. **Founder access.** Direct line for their VIP clients with complex situations. Priority roadmap input via a monthly 1-hour call.
6. **Case-study co-authorship rights.** Their partnership becomes one of the first 3 published success stories.

### Tier B — Standard Partners (agency #4 onwards)

Once the channel is proven and at least one case study is live, subsequent partners join on commercially simpler terms:

1. **3 months free Pro for every referred user.** No revenue share.
2. **Co-branded landing page** (same template, see below).
3. **Standard 12-month partnership contract**, renewable.
4. **No segment exclusivity.** Prevista reserves the right to onboard additional agencies in the same segment.
5. **Workshop available as negotiated add-on**, not default.

### Why this split works economically

- Revenue share scales badly: at realistic per-agency conversion, annual rev-share payouts are €150-500 — too small to motivate Tier-2 agencies past year 1, but a cash drag on Prevista at scale.
- The 3-month Pro trial **pays the channel cost in inventory (near-zero marginal cost), not cash**. The trial itself boosts paid conversion — typically 25-35% vs 12-18% from a free tier — so net revenue per agency rises even without rev share.
- Founding Partners get the upside they took risk for, in a way that's clearly justified to later partners ("they went first, pre-case-study"). Standard startup logic.

Net revenue impact at 5 partnerships:

| Model | Paying users | Gross ARR @ €120 | Rev-share out | Net to Prevista |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Original (rev share, all partners) | ~25 | €3,000 | ~€900 | **€2,100** |
| Revised (3 Founding + 2 Standard) | ~60 | €7,200 | ~€650 | **€6,550** |

Net roughly 3× higher under revised model, even on conservative trial-conversion assumptions.

### The 3-month free Pro offer — client guardrails

The offer is given to the *end user* (the assignee), not the agency. Guardrails:

- **Gated to verified Profile-A.** Signup must meet EU residence + age 35-60 check, via agency referral code. Prevents friends-and-family forwarding abuse.
- **Strictly time-bounded.** 90 days from signup. Auto-reverts to free tier on day 91 unless the user subscribes. Reminder emails at day 75 and day 88.
- **No reactivation.** If the user churns to free tier and returns later, no second trial.
- **Revisable for future cohorts.** Contract clause: *"Prevista may adjust the client benefit for new partnership cohorts."* Preserves the option to shorten the offer (e.g., to 2 months or 1 month) as product and retention mature.

### Co-branded landing page — what it delivers

Each agency gets a dedicated entry point: `prevista.com/relocation/[agency]`. This page delivers five things a generic `prevista.com` signup cannot:

1. **Trust transfer from agency → Prevista.** Assignee arrives via a URL that shows the agency they already trust (who just handled their housing, permit, tax number) vouching for Prevista. Typical conversion lift in partnership contexts: **20-40% vs generic landing pages**.
2. **Continuity of service.** Frames Prevista as *included in the relocation package* rather than *another app*. Changes the user's mental model from "cold pitch" to "service I'm already entitled to."
3. **Corridor-specific personalisation.** Hero copy matches the agency's dominant corridor ("Moving from France to Luxembourg?" vs generic "Pension clarity for expats"). 3-month Pro offer pre-applied, no code entry.
4. **Clean attribution.** Unique URL = unambiguous source tracking per partner. Critical once 5+ partners are live and renewal decisions need data.
5. **Sales asset for the agency.** The agency can show the co-branded page to their own corporate clients as proof of service differentiation — strengthens their commercial motion, which is a structural reason for them to stay engaged past year 1.

**Build discipline: one template, three variables.** Not five bespoke pages.

- Variable 1: agency logo + 1-line welcome copy signed by an agency partner
- Variable 2: corridor focus (FR→LU / DE→LU / UK→EU / EU→CH)
- Variable 3: promo code auto-applied (3-month Pro trial)

Initial template build: 4-6 hours of design/dev. Adding partner #10 should be a 15-minute config change, not a new page. Per-agency copy variations beyond these three variables are out of scope — discipline beats bespoke.

Tier 1 (global majors, month 9+) may negotiate fuller co-brand (joint logo lockup, richer bespoke copy) as a closing lever. For Tier 2/3, the three-variable template is the standing answer.

### What Prevista asks in return (unchanged across both tiers)

1. **Placement in the welcome pack** — day-1 email, onboarding checklist, PDF "move kit." This is the non-negotiable core ask.
2. **One paragraph of co-written copy** positioning Prevista as part of the agency's service, not a third-party tool.
3. **Intro call with 2-3 of their recent clients** to refine Profile-A understanding (optional, offered in year 1).
4. **Written permission to reference the partnership publicly** after 3 months of live integration.

## 5. Expected volumes per partnership

Assumptions for a Tier-2 agency handling 200 moves/year with co-branded landing page and 3-month Pro offer:

| Stage | Rate | Annual volume |
|---|---|---|
| Moves in agency's client flow | 100% | 200 |
| Assignees who open welcome pack / reach referral link | 60-70% | 130 |
| Assignees who click through to Prevista | 40-50% (lifted by co-branded page + concrete 3mo Pro offer vs generic "free tier") | 60 |
| Pro trial signups (Profile-A gate applied) | 60-75% of clickers | 40-50 |
| Trial → paid conversion (measured on day 91) | 25-35% (SaaS trial norms; generic free → paid would be 12-18%) | 10-15 |

**Per-agency steady-state (year 2+):** ~45 trial signups, ~12 paying.
**5 Tier-2 partnerships:** ~225 trial signups, ~60 paying/year.
**+1 Tier-1 landed (optional, month 9+):** roughly doubles the number.

First-year numbers will be lower due to ramp (agencies typically take 3-4 months to embed the referral into their workflow smoothly). Model ~60-70% of steady-state for year 1: ~135 trial signups, ~35 paying.

**Key uncertainty to validate in pilots:** the trial → paid conversion figure (25-35%). This is the single assumption with the largest revenue impact. The first two pilots should be instrumented to produce a clean measurement of this number by day 120 — it determines whether the "no rev share for Standard Partners" model holds, or whether Prevista needs to add a CPA element in the v2 terms.

## 6. The pitch

### What to lead with

Do not lead with product features. Lead with the agency's business problem:

> *"Your clients ask you about pensions 6 months after they land, when it's hardest to help. We're the tool that lets you answer that question on day 1 — at no cost to your service. It's already saving other LU relocators from the awkward 'I don't know, you should probably ask a tax advisor' moment."*

### The insight that actually sells

Relocation agencies are under margin pressure from HR-tech consolidation (Topia, Equus, AssignmentPro). Anything that **differentiates their service without adding headcount** is actively being evaluated. This is not a cold pitch — it's adjacent to an existing procurement conversation they are already having internally.

### Three objections and pre-built responses

**"Is this regulated financial advice?"**
→ "No. We hold a letter from the CSSF Innovation Hub confirming the tool is an orientation and projection service, not investment advice. Your clients still go to their tax advisor or wealth manager for decisions — we help them arrive at those conversations informed."

**"Our clients are trusting us with sensitive data — how do you handle it?"**
→ "Your clients sign up directly with us through your referral link. You never hand us any data. We're GDPR-clean and hosted in the EU. You can audit our data handling at any time."

**"What if our competitors also use you?"**
→ "As one of our first three Founding Partners, you get 18 months of segment exclusivity in exchange for going live pre-case-study. Standard partnerships — which start once the channel is proven — don't include exclusivity, but by then the differentiation has moved from *who uses us* to *how you use us* (co-branded workshops, founder access for your VIPs, custom onboarding copy)."

**"If later partners don't pay revenue share, why should we?"**
→ "You don't pay us — we pay you, for taking pilot risk before we had a case study. Standard terms (3-month free Pro for clients, no rev share) kick in after partner #3, because by then the commercial case is proven and risk has come out of it. You're being compensated for going first."

### The pilot proposal (template structure — Founding Partner terms)

- **Scope:** Next 30 assignees receive a Prevista referral link in their welcome pack, unlocking 3 months free Pro.
- **Duration:** 90 days from first integration. Conversion measured at day 120 (30 days post-trial for the last cohort).
- **What Prevista delivers:** Co-branded landing page, welcome-pack copy, weekly signup report, direct line for any client question during pilot, quarterly workshop.
- **What the agency delivers:** Integration of referral link, permission to reference partnership in case study after 3 months of live integration.
- **Success criteria:** ≥40% of 30 assignees sign up for the Pro trial, ≥25% trial-to-paid conversion at day 120, ≥2 positive qualitative client reports.
- **Post-pilot:** If criteria met, convert to formal Founding Partner contract (rev share + 18-month exclusivity + workshops). If not met, joint review to understand whether it's a placement problem, an offer problem, or a product-fit problem before extending or closing.

## 10. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| CSSF letter delayed | Channel stalls at first serious meeting; 1-3 months lost | Escalate CSSF Innovation Hub conversation *now*. Budget 3 months. Have a credible "pending confirmation" story until then. |
| GDPR concerns from agency legal | Partnership scope narrows or collapses | Design the referral flow as agency → client → Prevista (never agency hands us a list). Document the data flow in a one-pager legal can review in 30 min. |
| Revenue-share reconciliation overhead | Founder time drain by partner #3-4 | Tolt.io or Stripe Connect from day 1. Never a manual spreadsheet. |
| First pilot partner too small to produce meaningful data | Slow learning, weak case study | Prioritise partner selection for *volume over warmth*. Better a lukewarm 300-moves/year partner than a warm 40-moves/year one for the first pilot. |
| Agencies sign but never integrate properly | Paper partnership, no users | Include integration SLA in pilot agreement: "link live within 30 days of signing, or pilot clock doesn't start." Weekly check-in during first 30 days. |
| Over-reliance on one big partner | Concentration risk if they churn | Floor at 5 active partnerships before declaring the channel proven. Don't build product roadmap around any single partner's wishlist. |
| Regulatory scope creep | Agency asks for "advice" features that cross regulated-activity line | Keep the Prevista-agency boundary clean: we provide orientation, their clients' tax advisors / wealth managers provide advice. Train agency contacts to repeat this framing. |
| Trial → paid conversion underperforms (< 20%) | Standard Partner economics weaken; need to add CPA to keep agencies #4+ motivated | Measure precisely from pilot #1. If < 20% at day 120, activate v2 terms: add a small CPA (€3-8) on verified Profile-A trial signups for Standard Partners. Don't pay CPA on raw free signups — the Profile-A gate is what protects quality. |
| Standard Partners feel shortchanged vs Founding Partners | Pitch stalls with later agencies; partnership motion hits ceiling at 3 | Lead the pitch with the founding-vs-standard logic *explicitly* (they took pilot risk; that's what's being paid for). Show the pilot case study as evidence the commercial case is now proven. Do not hide the two-tier model. |
