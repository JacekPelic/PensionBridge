# Prevista — Design Context

## Users

Internationally-mobile professionals (expats) whose careers span multiple EU jurisdictions — typically 3+ countries (commonly FR, CH, LU, and surrounding EU/EFTA). Age range roughly 35–60. They use Prevista to unify fragmented pension data across state / workplace / personal pillars, surface the retirement-income gap, and plan decisions (retirement age, residency, product contributions).

**Context of use:** evening or weekend planning sessions. Pension gaps and retirement decisions are emotionally loaded — users arrive anxious or procrastinating. The tool's job is to absorb the complexity so the user feels a bit less small against it.

**Demo persona:** Mats Karlsson, 45, Luxembourg resident, career across France (10y), Switzerland (5y), Luxembourg (6y, ongoing). Goal: €5,500/mo at retirement. Current projection: ~€3,840/mo. Gap to close.

## Brand Personality

**Calm · authoritative · precise.**

The reference point is a private bank or actuary's office — institutional trust built through restraint, typographic precision, and the absence of sales-theatre. Not cold or bureaucratic (that's the pension office we're replacing), but composed. Warmth comes from clarity, not from illustration or copy tricks.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Composed, not decorated.** Typography and spatial rhythm do the work. Avoid glassmorphism, gradient text, glowing accents, decorative sparklines.
- **Editorial precision.** Think long-form financial journalism or a well-laid-out annual report — dense when needed, but with generous whitespace, confident hierarchy, and numbers that feel measured.
- **Sparing colour.** Gold as the singular accent over navy or warm-neutral surfaces. Colour reserved for state (verified, pending, alert). No multi-hue gradients.
- **Both themes first-class.** Light and dark each get their own calm palette. Neither is the afterthought. Dark should not lean "trader/gaming"; light should not lean "consumer fintech".

### Anti-references (what Prevista is NOT)

- **Generic SaaS dashboard** — purple-to-blue gradients, card grids, AI color palette, glassmorphism, gradient text.
- **Robinhood / trading app** — dark + neon green, speculative energy, gamified progression.
- **Consumer banking (Revolut/N26)** — overly playful, big illustrations, bright brand colours chasing attention.
- **Government portal** — stiff, form-heavy, cold, joyless.

## Design Principles

1. **Verified over decorative.** Every number should be traceable to a source (picture / document / engine). No chart is decoration; if a sparkline doesn't convey something real, remove it.
2. **Restraint as trust.** Private-bank aesthetic. Generous whitespace, fewer type sizes with more contrast, sparing accent use. Confidence > flourish.
3. **Both themes equally considered.** Design, test and polish light and dark in parallel. Neutrals tint toward the brand hue in both.
4. **Calm under complex data.** The app has already absorbed the complexity. Surface one thing at a time; progressive disclosure over cramming every number into the first view.
5. **Precision in numbers.** Tabular-nums for all currency/year/age figures. Consistent currency + locale formatting. No hand-waving approximations unless explicitly labelled.

## Accessibility

**WCAG AA baseline:** body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, interactive elements ≥ 3:1, full keyboard operation, visible focus, semantic landmarks, labelled form inputs.
